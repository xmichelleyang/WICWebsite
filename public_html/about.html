What does WIC do?

  • Hold technical events

    We bring in industry professionals to hold tech talks and workshops, including all-female engineer panels. Bring your resumes!

  • Get you into Grad School

    Our grad WIC members hold seminars on getting you into the program you want.

  • Attend Conferences

    WIC attends regional and national conferences for women in computing, like Grace Hopper, CWIC and init(together).

  • Programming Competitions

    We hold a beginner's programming competition every quarter, restricted to students who have yet to take upper division classes. More info

Undergraduate Officers

President

Angelique De Castro

Angelique is a fourth-year Computer Science major. She joined WIC to meet and support other programmers like herself in the community. Yet, not only did she gain that, she also discovered new ways to network and develop her interests in the field of computing and beyond. Currently, Angelique is interested in developing educational technology and resources. Her hobbies include reading, writing, playing the guitar, and daydreaming about hedgehogs.

Vice President

Lucy Chian

Lucy is a third-year majoring in Computer Science with a specialization in bioinformatics. She's particularly interested in the application of CS to interdisciplinary problems, especially biology. Since she joined in her first year, WIC has given her invaluable opportunities within the CS community and has proved to be a fantastic support group. Outside of CS, Lucy plays soccer on the UCSD club team and enjoys cooking/eating, photography, and the outdoors.

Treasurer

Revan Jafar

Revan is a fourth-year Math-Computer Science major with a minor in Business. She joined WiC to be a part of a welcoming community, find opportunities and gain experience. Her favorite part of computer science is working on hands-on team projects and being able to build solutions to help others, not to mention the limitless possibilities of technology! Her other interests include all things chocolate, making people laugh, watching too much TV, and going on adventures! She also loves meeting new people so don't be shy, say hi, cause she's super fly, and will teach you some computer sci!

Secretary

Tiffany Truong

Tiffany is a fourth-year Computer Science major. She was initially interested in Computer Science because of the widespread impact it had in the world and how it was used in such widely different fields, from finance to transportation. One of the things she enjoys about Computer Science is the fact that it's always changing, constantly innovating and improving. In her free time, Tiffany likes to sketch, travel, watch films, and kayak.

Professional Development Co-Chair

Lea Lacson

Lea is a fourth-year Computer Science major. She joined WIC to be surrounded by the inspiring community that they have and to help spread opportunities for others to explore Computer Science. When she is not programming, Lea is busy dancing as captain of the on-campus competitive hip hop dance team Ascension.

Professional Development Co-Chair

Xiaohui Tong

Tong is a fourth year computer science major . She is interested in bridging the gap between technology and people. She is currently doing research in design, which allows her to apply her technical skills in various areas. Tong is also a lead career peer at Career Services Center. She enjoys helping other students to achieve their career success. When she is not programming or talking with students, she likes reading and writing.

Technical Skills Co-Chair

Jace Chan

Jace is a second-year undergraduate computer science student at UC San Diego. He supports the presence of women in computing and encourages conversations that explore the intersection of privilege, identity, and technology. He believes all women deserve an equal opportunity to succeed, and joined WIC in order to amplify the voices of women in the computational sciences. In addition to his enthusiasm for outreach, he believes in leveraging the power of technology for social good.

Technical Skills Co-Chair

Qiqi is a second-year Computer Science Major. She was initially dawn to the major because of her interest in technology, and the infinite possibilities that a computer can offer. She joined WiC because she wanted to meet other members who share the same passion in the field. When she is not stuck in the basement, Qiqi enjoys watching movies, reading, shopping, and going to the beach. Qiqi is also a huge foodie, who loves to take pictures of her food.

Outreach Chair

Jessica Yang

Jessica is a third-year computer science major at UCSD. She is currently the Outreach Chair for Women in Computing, and works to encourage high school females to code. In her free time, she enjoys crocheting and watching TV.

Programming Competition Chair

Jessica is a third-year in Computer Science who aspires to spread her love of blobular unicorns to the world through her software engineering career. She has loved problem solving ever since she was but a young child. Her journey in Computer Science, however, began with AP CS in her sophomore year of high school. She's always loved programming competitions, and wants everyone else to have as much fun as she has had! Having hatched great plans, she is ridiculously excited about bringing especially fun programming competitions to the competitors this year. Outside of programming, she loves to draw, craft, laugh, eat, and personify a potato.

What does WIC do?

  • Hold technical events

    We bring in industry professionals to hold tech talks and workshops, including all-female engineer panels. Bring your resumes!

  • Get you into Grad School

    Our grad WIC members hold seminars on getting you into the program you want.

  • Attend Conferences

    WIC attends regional and national conferences for women in computing, like Grace Hopper, CWIC and init(together).

  • Programming Competitions

    We hold a beginner's programming competition every quarter, restricted to students who have yet to take upper division classes. More info

Undergraduate Officers

Since she joined in her first year, WIC has given her invaluable opportunities within the CS community and has proved to be a fantastic support group. Outside of CS, Lucy plays soccer and enjoys cooking/eating, photography, and the outdoors. She recently returned from studying abroad in Copenhagen.

Vice President

Jessica Yang

Jessica is a fourth-year Computer Science major. Her passion in computer science stems from the unlimited applications of programming to different areas of interests. When she first joined WIC freshman year, she was simply looking for a community to be a part of; she ended up staying because of the people she's met along the way and because she became increasingly passionate about the cause. Outside of programming, Jessica likes to watch TV shows, play video games, try new foods, and crochet.

Treasurer

Shreya Malik

Shreya is a fourth year bioinformatics major minoring in business. She has experience in performing algorithmic quantitative analysis in the molecular engineering lab at UCSD and has successfully completed several summer internships at companies of various sizes. Her academic pursuits place her at the most innovative crossroads between computer science, biotechnology, and consulting. She hopes to use these experiences to further WiC's goals by inspiring growth, networking opportunities, and overall learning through her contributions as treasurer for the 2016-17 academic year. She's always willing to answer questions, so feel free to approach her with anything!

Technical Skills Co-Chair

Qiqi Wu

Qiqi is a third-year Computer Science Major. She was initially dawn to the major because of her interest in technology, and the infinite possibilities that a computer can offer. She joined WiC because she wanted to meet other members who share the same passion in the field. Her goal is to empower other students to succeed and to encourage inclusion in computing. When she is not stuck in the lab, Qiqi enjoys watching movies, reading, shopping, and going to the beach.

Technical Skills Co-Chair

Jace Chan

Jace is a third-year undergraduate computer science student at UC San Diego. He supports the presence of women in computing and encourages conversations that explore the intersection of privilege, identity, and technology. He believes all women deserve an equal opportunity to succeed, and joined WIC in order to amplify the voices of women in the computational sciences. In addition to his enthusiasm for outreach, he believes in leveraging the power of technology for social good.

Outreach Chair

Vrushali Samant

Vrushali is a third-year undergraduate computer science major at UC San Diego. She took an interest in computer science thanks to a programming class in her high school, and hopes to encourage other high school girls to develop a passion for the subject by reaching out to them through various events hosted at UCSD. She joined WIC to connect with other people with similar interests and to become a part of the incredible CS community, and so part of her other time is spent in bringing together students interested in CS through socials and other fun activities. Outside of CS, she loves reading, sketching, and eating!

From there, she was hooked on the logic and problem solving skills associated with the field. Outside of CS and programming, she enjoys drawing and being a huge foodie.

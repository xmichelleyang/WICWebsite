Tech Corner

Industry:

CES (the Consumer Electronics Show), where tech company giants and new startups unveiled new products in the year to come, was held earlier this month in Las Vegas. Highlights include a huge breakout of wearables, ranging from a virtual reality headset with the Crystal Cove, fully immersing your gaming experience, to smart baby onesies called The Mimo Baby from Intel that might be the baby monitor of the future.

Intel may be in the talks with Verizon to hand over its Web TV Project.

Apple releases iOS 7.1 Beta 3, tweaking the interface and also acquires rapid-fire camera app developer SnappyLabs.

Facebook has developed a new mobile A/B testing framework, Airlock, which will help improve user experience on its mobile apps.

Microsoft announced they will be releasing the next generation Windows, "Threshold," in spring 2015, and is still on the search for its new CEO.

Google is teaming up with Audi, GM, Honda, Hyundai and Nvidia to bring Android to cars as early as this year.

CS @ UCSD:

Setting out to simulate all rainbows found in nature wound up with an "almost complete picture of how rainbows form." UCSD alumnus, Iman Sadeghi, his advisor CS professor Henrik Wann Jensen, and scientists from Spain, England, and Switzerland, published their findings in ACM Transactions on Graphics in December.
